Bangladeshi Cuisine

It is mainly based on rice and fish, reflecting the riverine geography and rich culture. Spicy curries, bhartas, khichuri and hilsa fish are the main attractions here. There is also Mughal influence in biryani and kebabs, and among sweets, Bogra yogurt and pitha-puli are very popular

Daily Foods

  • 1.Rice is the staple food in Bangladeshi cuisine, often served with a variety of curries and side dishes.
  • 2.Freshwater fish (especially Hilsa/Ilish) and various meats, often prepared as curries, bhorta, or bhaji are often served with rice.
  • 3.Bhortas are essential, comforting Bangladeshi side dishes made by mashing vegetables, fish, or dried fish with pungent mustard oil, onions, garlic, and chilies.
  • 4.Bhaji (stir-fried vegetables) is a vital part of daily Bangladeshi cuisine, commonly served alongside rice and lentil soup (dal) to create a balanced meal.
  • 5.Daal (lentils) is an indispensable, everyday staple in Bangladeshi cuisine, usually served as a nutrient-dense, soupy accompaniment to steamed rice.

    • Sweets

    Bangladeshi cuisine features a rich variety of milk-based and syrup-soaked sweets, with staples including Chomchom, Rasmalai, Mishti Doi (sweet yogurt), and Jilapi.

    Street Foods

    While, its vibrant street food scene features savory, spicy snacks like Fuchka (stuffed pastry with tamarind water), Jhalmuri (spicy puffed rice), and Samosas/Shingara are widely well-known, foreigners are very interested to come to Bangladesh for taking their tastes.
